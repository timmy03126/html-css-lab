Tapir Fun Facts!

  • The closest relatives to a tapir are the rhino and horse!
  • Tapirs are excellent swimmers!
  • Tapirs can eat 75 pounds of food per day!
  • A group of tapirs are called a candle!

Types of Tapirs

Type Information Where Are They Found What They Look Like
Malayan Tapir Malayan tapirs are sometimes called “Oreo” tapirs because of their distinctive black and white color pattern resembles an Oreo cookie. The black on the front and back with white or gray in the middle is a form of camouflage that breaks up the tapir’s outline in the shadows of the forest. The stripes and spots on the vulnerable babies help them blend into the dappled sunlight and leaf shadows of the forest and protects them from predators. Myanmar (Burma) and Thailand south to Malaysia and Sumatra.
Mountain Tapir Though it is the smallest (and furriest!) of tapir species, the Mountain tapir is the largest mammal in the tropical Andes mountain range. Their long hair is brownish to black, and their lips are lined in a white color. Adults have two characteristic hair-free patches on their rump. Colombia, Ecuador and northern Peru.
Baird's Tapir The Baird’s tapir is found in Oaxaca Province in Mexico through parts of Central America, including the western side of the Andean mountain range in Colombia. Baird’s tapirs live in rainforests, lower montane forests, deciduous forests, flooded grasslands and marsh areas. The Baird’s tapir is found in Oaxaca Province in Mexico through parts of Central America.
Lowland Tapir The Lowland tapir can be identified by its large stiff mane or crest from forehead to shoulder, which the other three species of tapirs do not have. They are also known for being strong swimmers, with the ability to cross rivers and take to the water to escape predators. South America
